Program Spotlight — LIFT Mentor Match

Citrus College, the representative college in the multi-college, multi-region LIFT Alliance, is launching a Mentor Match Program on the Mentor & Match – Professional Platform. It pairs employees across classifications and campuses – classified professionals, part-time and full-time faculty, and administrators – to expand equitable access to mentorship, leadership development, and career advancement.

GOAL 1Equitable Access to Mentorship

Expand equitable access to professional development through mentorship across all employee classifications at the multi-college, multi-region LIFT Alliance institutions.

Activities

  • Launch the Mentor Match Program: planning, organization, and coordination across the alliance.
  • Summer 2026 soft launch with leadership teams; troubleshoot platform access.
  • Develop marketing, mentor/mentee training curriculum, and schedules.
  • Year 1 launch with info sessions, marketing, and training; the Campus Lead informs all employees via senates, manager & staff meetings, PD committees, and HR.
  • Year 2 relaunch with “Culture of Care” Awards, enhanced outreach, and advanced mentoring training.
  • Create refined Best Practices Toolkits (“Top Tips & FAQs from Users”) and report platform metrics each fall and spring term.

Measurable Outcomes & Assessment

  1. 01
    Employee participation across faculty, administrators, and classified professionals, growing from Year 1 to Year 2.
    AssessmentPlatform analytics; term reports by college/alliance.
  2. 02
    Participants complete mentor and/or mentee training modules.
    AssessmentTraining completion data.
  3. 03
    Refined Best Practices Toolkit published by Year 2 from user feedback.
    AssessmentPublished toolkit; user FAQ contributions.
GOAL 2Culture of Belonging

Cultivate a systemwide culture of belonging, mutual support, and inclusive leadership.

Activities

  • Regional Meet-Ups: annual statewide virtual meetups and three regional in-person meet-ups, encouraging participants to serve as both mentor and mentee.
  • Culture of Care Awards recognizing excellence in inclusive mentorship for colleges and individuals.
  • Introduce and publish the Mentorship Reflection Journal for participants to share impact and transformation stories.

Measurable Outcomes & Assessment

  1. 01
    Participants serving as both mentor and mentee, reinforcing reciprocal learning.
    AssessmentPlatform analytics.
  2. 02
    Mentored participants report increased career confidence and goal clarity.
    AssessmentParticipant surveys & focus groups.
  3. 03
    Mentoring stories published per college in the Mentorship Reflection Journal.
    AssessmentDigital Journal Collection.
GOAL 3Scalable, Equity-Driven Model

Build a scalable, measurable, and equity-driven open-access model that advances EEO objectives.

Activities

  • Data collection & program improvement: built-in platform analytics produce semester dashboards tracking match outcomes, participation across roles, and demographic inclusion.
  • Conduct participant surveys (Spring) tracking career confidence, goal clarity, intent to stay, and aspirations for advancement.
  • Host a Virtual Mentorship Impact Showcase & Summit after the impact study; colleges share learnings and co-design the next evolution.
  • Develop a Program Improvement and Expansion Plan.

Measurable Outcomes & Assessment

    Part-time employee participation, with targeted outreach to reduce access barriers.

As the representative college in the multi-college, multi-region LIFT Alliance, Citrus College launches a Mentor Match Program on the Mentor & Match – Professional Platform. The initiative pairs employees across classifications, colleges, and regions — classified professionals, part-time and full-time faculty, and administrators — to expand equitable access to mentorship, leadership development, and career advancement.

Three Project Goals
From the application workplan, Section 2.1
GOAL 1Equitable Access

Expand equitable access to professional development through mentorship across all employee classifications, embedding mentorship as a right for every employee — especially those historically excluded from leadership pipelines.

GOAL 2Culture of Belonging

Cultivate a systemwide culture of belonging, mutual support, and inclusive leadership where every classified professional, faculty member, and administrator can contribute and grow.

GOAL 3Scalable Model

Build a scalable, measurable, equity-driven open-access model on the Mentor & Match Platform that advances EEO objectives and becomes a model for the entire CCC system.

Why This Project
Problem statement · Section 1.1

At Citrus College, classified professionals are often excluded from EEO-funded initiatives and underrepresented in leadership and decision-making roles, and many part-time staff — particularly evening staff — are excluded from professional development by scheduling conflicts. Part-time and early-career faculty lack mentorship to navigate institutional culture or pursue leadership, and administrators frequently operate in professional silos with few opportunities for peer reflection. The Mentor Match Program addresses these gaps with a structured, equity-minded, technology-enabled mentoring model for every employee group.
